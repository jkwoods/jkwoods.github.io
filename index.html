Jess Woods

I'm a (last year!) PhD student in Computer and Information Science at the University of Pennsylvania, advised by Sebastian Angel in the Security and Privacy Lab (within the Distributed Systems Lab). I am excited to start as an instructor at Arcadia Unviersity this fall! Students signing up for my classes should feel free to reach out to me with questions at woodsj (at) arcadia.edu.

Research wise, I'm interested in applied cryptography and it's crossover with programming languages. My recent work has been in compilers for zero-knowledge proofs. I'm also passionate about making math/computer science education better and more accessible. I'm happy to talk about any of my papers/accompanying code - especially if you are using my arithmetic circuit code or trying to build/integrate similar code into your project. Questions about my research should be directed to woodsjk (at) seas.upenn.edu.
